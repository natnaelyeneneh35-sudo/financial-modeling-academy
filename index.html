Module 7: Valuation Modeling

The Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model is the intrinsic valuation workhorse of Wall Street. It values a company based on the present value of its future free cash flows.

The DCF Formula

The enterprise value is calculated using the following formula:

$$DCF = \sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{CF_t}{(1+WACC)^t} + \frac{TV}{(1+WACC)^n}$$
  • CF_t: Unlevered Free Cash Flow in year $t$
  • WACC: Weighted Average Cost of Capital (Discount Rate)
  • TV: Terminal Value

Interactive Toolkit: WACC Calculator

Use this tool to calculate the Weighted Average Cost of Capital:
